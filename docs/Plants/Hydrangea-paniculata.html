Hydrangea paniculata

Common Names: Panicle Hydrangea, Peegee Hydrangea, Hardy Hydrangea, Limelight Hydrangea

Notes on the Plant

From Better Homes and Garden's 1997 book, New Complete Guide to Gardening:

...[P]eegee hydrangea(H. paniculata 'Grandiflora') is a large shrub or small tree that produces pointed clusters of creamy white flowers, which are 1 to 1.5 feet long, at its branch tips. These weigh down the branches, forming a lovely weeping effect. The showy clusters open greenish white and mature to creamy white, then age to a beautiful shade of old rose. Upright to weeping; 15 to 20 feet tall and half as wide. Best as single specimen in mixed border; combine with lower plants that complement the weeping shape.

Care and Features:

  • Plant container plants in spring or fall. Space peegee hydrangeas 6 feet or more apart. Softwood cuttings root easily.
  • Cut flowers of peegee hydrangea when they fade to improve winter appearance, if desired.
  • Peegee hydrangea blooms on new growth. Produces largest blossoms if pruned yearly in early spring before growth begins. Cut back main branches to just above lowest pair or two of budes. If left unpruned, flowers are more numerous but smaller.
  • Give peegee hydrangea full sun to part shade.
  • Tolerates any well-drained soil with a pH below 7.
  • Zones 4-8; tolerates urban sites.
